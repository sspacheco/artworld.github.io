Michelangelo Buonarroti

1475 - 1564

Michelangelo Buonarroti (1475-1564) was a towering figure of the Italian Renaissance, renowned for his profound impact on sculpture, painting, and architecture. Born in Caprese, Italy, Michelangelo showed artistic promise early on, eventually studying under the patronage of the Medici family in Florence. His masterpieces include the marble statue David and the Pietà. Michelangelo’s work embodies intense emotion, precision, and a pursuit of ideal beauty, solidifying his legacy as a master of the Renaissance.

Artistic Style and Influence

Michelangelo Buonarroti’s style epitomizes the High Renaissance, blending idealized beauty, anatomical precision, and dramatic expression. His deep understanding of human form allowed him to create powerful, muscular figures that convey both strength and emotional depth. Influenced by classical Greek and Roman art, Michelangelo sought perfection in proportion and realism, while his use of light and shadow added sculptural quality to his paintings. His intense, dynamic compositions foreshadowed Mannerism, influencing later artists and shaping Baroque expressiveness. Michelangelo’s legacy endures as a symbol of artistic mastery, merging physical beauty with profound emotion.
