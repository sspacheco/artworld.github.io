Njideka Akunyili Crosby

1983

Njideka Akunyili Crosby (b. 1983) is a Nigerian-born, Los Angeles-based artist renowned for her mixed-media paintings that explore cultural hybridity and identity. Growing up in Nigeria, Crosby moved to the United States at 16, an experience that profoundly influences her work. She attended Swarthmore College and the Yale School of Art, where she developed her signature style, combining painting, collage, and photo transfers. Crosby’s work delves into the nuances of navigating multiple cultures, portraying intimate, everyday moments that reflect her Nigerian heritage and life in America.

Artistic Style and Influence

Njideka Akunyili Crosby’s work is deeply influenced by her Nigerian upbringing, as well as her experiences living in the United States. She draws inspiration from her personal life, art history, and popular culture, combining Nigerian and Western aesthetics to explore cultural identity. Artists such as Romare Bearden, whose collage work also explores identity and heritage, and Kerry James Marshall, known for depicting Black life, have shaped her approach. Her work reflects themes of memory, tradition, and the complexities of diasporic life, using photo transfers to blend personal and cultural history.

Quotes

“There’s a particular perspective that I bring, coming from the culture that I come from, having seen things that I’ve seen.”
