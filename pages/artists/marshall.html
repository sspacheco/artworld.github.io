Kerry James Marshall

1955

Kerry James Marshall (b. 1955) is an American painter renowned for his powerful depictions of Black life and culture. Born in Birmingham, Alabama, and later raised in Los Angeles, Marshall’s experiences of the Civil Rights Movement and the Watts Rebellion shaped his worldview and commitment to representing African American identity. He studied at the Otis College of Art and Design, where he developed a style that blends realism and abstraction, exploring themes of identity, history, and social narratives. Marshall’s work aims to address the lack of Black representation in art history and celebrate Black beauty, resilience, and culture.

Artistic Style and Influence

Marshall’s work is heavily influenced by art history, particularly the European masters, but with a critical eye toward their exclusion of Black narratives. He draws inspiration from artists like Rembrandt and Édouard Manet, using classical techniques and compositions to insert Black figures into scenes from which they’ve historically been absent. The Black Power movement and the Harlem Renaissance also significantly influenced his work, as he seeks to reclaim and celebrate Black identity in spaces where it has been marginalized.
