Julie Mehretu

1970

Julie Mehretu (b. 1970) is an Ethiopian American artist known for her large-scale abstract paintings that layer architectural elements with abstract forms. Born in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, and raised in Michigan, she studied at Kalamazoo College and the Rhode Island School of Design. Mehretu’s work explores themes of migration, urbanization, social upheaval, and the complexity of the human experience. Her multi-layered compositions reflect her interest in mapping and cities, symbolizing the dynamic, interconnected nature of the modern world.

Artistic Style and Influence

Julie Mehretu is heavily influenced by architectural and cartographic imagery, as well as the Abstract Expressionist movement, particularly artists like Jackson Pollock and Cy Twombly. She draws inspiration from urban environments and sociopolitical issues, using her art to represent the complexity of human history and geography. Her work also engages with the idea of "place" and how space and memory intersect, often evoking feelings of dislocation, migration, and resilience.

Quotes
